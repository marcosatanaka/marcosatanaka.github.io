What’s New in Play

Version 2.3.0

Released September 16, 2024


Play 2.3.0 adds support for the latest features introduced in iOS and iPadOS 18. It offers controls for Control Center, includes new app icons that match your Home Screen style, and deeply integrates with Spotlight. It also brings new forms of interaction via App Shortcuts that allows you to add and retrieve data using Siri, Spotlight, the Shortcuts app, and more. And you can now translate video descriptions to your preferred language. Check out the dedicated page to learn more about the new features introduced in this update.

Version 2.2.5

Released September 06, 2024


• New setting for inline video playback on iPhone. Go to Settings → Video Player → Auto Enter Fullscreen to configure.

• New button to watch in Picture in Picture (PiP) mode. By default, PiP automatically starts when you swipe away a fullscreen video, but you can now also activate it from the player’s toolbar (Player Settings → Enter PiP).

• iPad now uses the native system player when watching in fullscreen.

• iPad now takes advantage of all available space to display video details and the inline player.

• iPad now properly supports Picture in Picture (PiP) mode.

• The player now saves your playback position when exiting the detail view as well as when pausing the video.

• The modal video details screen (available only when “Tap to Watch” is enabled in Settings → Video List) now uses the app’s tint color.

Version 2.0.0

Released Nov 27, 2023


Play 2.0 brings the ability to follow YouTube channels and get new videos as they're released. You can save the ones you want to watch, and delete the rest. It integrates harmoniously with the existing feature set, allowing you to have a complete YouTube experience within the app - get new videos, save, organize, and watch without algorithms!


It also adds the ability to create folders for Tags and Smart Searches, giving you more ways to catalog and organize your videos.


These features are part of Play Premium, a subscription designed for features that extend Play's capabilities beyond saving videos for later. It brings a more sustainable business model to the app and allows me to invest in more advanced features, ensuring the app's long-term development.
