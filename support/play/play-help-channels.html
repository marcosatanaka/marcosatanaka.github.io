About

Play was designed to help users save and organize videos to watch later. The goal is to open the app and quickly start watching a video instead of choosing from countless options generated by an algorithmic feed.

But sometimes, you may want to save many videos from a specific channel at once. This custom shortcut helps you to do that.

Downloading the Shortcut

Click the button below to add the shortcut to your Shortcuts app:

In the Shortcuts app, click the button Add Shortcut to complete.

You can now open the shortcut to see and customize its actions or run it by hovering your mouse over the icon and clicking on the play button that appears.

Getting the Channel ID

The shortcut asks for the channel ID, a unique text that identifies each YouTube channel. If you already know the ID of the channel you want to add, skip to the next step.

There are several ways to get the ID of a channel, but the easiest way I found was by using this website. Just paste the channel's URL or username in the text field and click the Get YouTube Channel ID button:

Copy the channel ID that appears:

Running the Shortcut

In the Shortcuts app, hover your mouse over the recently added shortcut, and click on the play button that appears.

Paste the channel ID you obtained in the previous section and click Done.

The shortcut will show you a list of the last ten videos uploaded to this channel (you can customize this number by editing the shortcut and changing the parameter). It automatically removes videos you already have in Play, so only new videos are shown. Select the ones that you want to save and click Done.

That's it! The Shortcuts app will save the videos for later in Play. This tutorial shows the Shortcuts app running on the Mac, but it works the same on the iPhone and iPad.

Tip: Change the shortcut to use a fixed channel ID for channels you watch frequently. You can also change it to iterate over a list of channels for saving new videos from your channels at once.
